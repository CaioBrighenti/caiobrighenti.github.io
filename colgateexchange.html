Colgate Exchange

August 2017 - Present
Previous Next

Colgate Exchange is an ongoing group project I am undertaking in my project-based design thinking class at Colgate University, called the Design Lab. Design thinking is a human-centered process for problem solving and the class’ objective is to find any problem, on a reasonable scale, and use the design thinking process to create a potential solution for it. Loosely speaking, my group of five students is seeking to minimize both the needless acquisition and waste of durable goods on the Colgate campus, by connecting people who have things with people who want things.

To do this, we are designing a mobile application we call “Colgate Exchange.” The app is a hybrid of many popular buy, sell, and exchange services, such as Craigslist, Ebay, and Letgo. We want to connect people within the Colgate University community that have things with people that want things. Sellers list goods they no longer want and buyers sift through those seeking out things that may be useful to them. For instance, incoming freshmen seeking to purchase textbooks for required introductory courses could easily purchase them from older students. Our app would simply connect buyers with sellers, much in the way Craigslist does, and would not serve as transactional middle-man.

I, along with all four other group members, have designed this project from the ground up using the design thinking process. This is an ongoing project, and we are currently in the prototyping phase as of late November. I have designed the user interface for our application and some of the mockups I’ve created are shown on the left. These are simply design mockups created in Adobe Photoshop, a program with which I have extensive experience; we have not yet implemented any aspects of the application. This project has served to further develop my skills in design thinking, as well as my user experience design knowledge.
